• PUNY is the PERL language special interest group under the aegis of the NY Software Industry Association
  • Our purpose is bring the value of PERL to the business and educational communities of New York through classes and technical presentations
  • We will also develop active interest it the future of PERL to generate financial support and in-kind development efforts from large institutions and corporations.

There are very strong voices in the wilderness calling for changes in OS's as well as new varieties of perl so much so that both their directions will be forever altered. Already I can see several kinds Perl, nano, micro, single threading, multithreading and Perl/OS.

PERL has a utilitarian philosophy which has allowed it to grow to support an unlimited variety of operations, data storage, and documentation needs on virtually every platform. PUNY seeks to embolden perl to stand on its own both as an applications development language and an OS interface.

Linux has been finding its way onto tens of millions of desktops in every part of the world, yet we who have been using Unix style systems for a decade or so have found that is has actually become worn with time.

The world of users will soon realize that their new Linux OS is a mature server system and not necessarily a handy tool to query their universe of knowledge.

Every PUNY member must get specific benefits from the SIG and regional code and OS releases will be designed for the needs and desires of all our members. The need for Perl in business, education and development is so strong that any OS, including the Win32 family, will be welcomed.

This "PUNY release" will be constructed by SIG members who have skills, but will be seen as a black box by users. PUNY2 will be that new generation possibly in a modular h/w configuration.

PUNY will be integrated with work groups who will maintain a web neighborhood constructed wholly of Perl. The C++Sig and Perl Mongers have pledged to support by adapting their own classes and technical meetings for NYSIA members. Other publicly available languages, such as Lisp variants, will be discussed as they relate to systems integration.

The web goal is to create a community that would attract high school and college students as well as our peers constructed wholly of Perl. It will start as a thinclient desktop with such tools as mail, group ware, and data mining and storage. With time, probably very little, it will grow into a full-blown broadband service specifically designed the way we would like. It would also be a tremendously valuable asset.
