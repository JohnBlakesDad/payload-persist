Last Meeting, Feb 22nd

Thank you all for coming, there were about 20 future and experience perlers in attendance.

I feel our first meeting was a huge success. In fact, the very next morning Denny was contacted by the NYPC group to host the Linux Sig.

PUNY will be at NYPC (and other) install fests in full force to assure that perl is compiled and installed correctly on everybody's machines.

My 5-minute perl class stretched for two hours, but I am very hopeful that I gave the attendees the building blocks to get started on their own perl projects.

We then proceeded to a Q+A period where David (perl monger) and I fielded some really tough questions.

I gave the lecture fully aware that I was going to overreach my goal of teaching basic syntax, but a major responsibility of an "evangelist" is try to make people aware of the full power of perl's unique and powerful object oriented syntax. I reached my goal of giving the full tour of complex structures, class packages and persistent objects using only basic code snippets. I just hope I did not lose too many along the way J

I also introduced the PUNY release of Debian Linux and the LibOS, but not as deeply as I hoped though I must admit that I have not been able to work on these projects as much as I have wanted.

I modified my lecture on the fly and I will be encapsulating what I learned into class notes. With the help of others and possibly forays into other languages, we will very likely distribute our version of HOW-TO documents.

Thanks again for coming, and we hope to see you at our future events.
